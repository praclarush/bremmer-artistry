# Product

## Register

brand

## Platform

web

## Users

Prospective buyers and collectors: people deciding whether a piece is worth purchasing,
commissioning, or worth a trip to see in person. They arrive via word of mouth, social media, or a
past show, and browse with a specific question in mind: is this artist's work good, and can I go
see or get more of it? The admin area exists only to keep the public site's content current for
this audience; it is not a user-facing surface in its own right.

## Product Purpose

A public site for a working potter that documents pieces and process together (the "Pottery
Journal") rather than presenting a static product catalog, alongside an events calendar so people
can find and attend shows or workshops. Success is discovery and outreach: more people finding the
work and acting on it, primarily by showing up to see it in person.

## Positioning

It's a working journal, not a catalog — pieces and process are documented together, dated and
searchable, so a visitor sees the making as much as the finished piece.

## Conversion & proof

- Primary CTA: come see the work in person — find and attend an upcoming show or workshop.
- Secondary CTA: browse the Gallery / Pottery Journal at their own pace if not ready to attend.
- The line a visitor remembers after 10 seconds: this is handmade, working craft — not
  mass-produced.
- Belief ladder: this is real, handmade work, not mass-produced → there's an ongoing, documented
  body of work worth seeing → there's a specific upcoming opportunity to see it in person → that
  opportunity is easy to find and plan around (date, venue, details all in one place).
- Proof on hand: none yet. The documented body of work is the credibility signal for this phase —
  don't invent testimonials, press, or logos to fill the gap.

## Brand Personality

Warm, tactile, handmade — the site should feel like an extension of the work itself, not a
storefront wrapped around it.

## Anti-references

Explicitly not a cold corporate or SaaS-template gallery: no stock hero-metric layouts, no
tech-startup gloss, no gradient-and-glass polish standing between the visitor and the pottery.

## Design Principles

- Show the making, not just the made — pieces and process stay documented together; resist
  collapsing the Journal back into a plain product catalog.
- Warmth without kitsch — handmade and tactile, but never corporate-SaaS on one side or
  craft-fair-cute on the other.
- The event is the front door — in-person attendance is the primary conversion; the Gallery and
  Journal are the fallback path for visitors not ready to commit to that yet.
- Let the work carry the credibility — no manufactured social proof; the consistency and quality
  of the documented pieces does the persuading.
- Design for a walk-up decision, not a funnel — buyers and collectors are deciding in the moment
  they land, not being nurtured through a drip campaign.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Standard WCAG AA baseline; no additional known requirements beyond that.
